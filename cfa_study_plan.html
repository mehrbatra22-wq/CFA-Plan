⟡ Strategy Notes for Your Profile
  • Since you're forgetful, the weekly maintenance q-bank sessions aren't optional — they're the most important thing here. Even 15 questions on a strong topic does more than re-reading it.
  • Keep a running error log (a simple notes doc is fine). Every time you get something wrong, write down WHY — wrong concept, misread the question, or calculation error. Review this log weekly.
  • For FSA: build a one-page adjustments cheat sheet as you study each sub-topic. Reviewing this one page every few days costs 5 minutes and significantly slows forgetting.
  • Don't take all 3 mocks back-to-back without recovery time. Space them out and genuinely review before moving to the next one — mocks without review are almost worthless.
  • Ethics tends to be the tiebreaker at the borderline. The CFA Institute has historically given benefit of the doubt to candidates who score well in Ethics, so treat it as a priority, not an afterthought.
  • If you find a week going off-track, don't abandon the plan — just compress. Cut maintenance from 20 Qs to 10, but never skip it entirely.
